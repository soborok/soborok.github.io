Paring and Feedback

November 9, 2014

Learning from others and myself

In DBC I am doing more pairings than ever. I pair remotely and sometimes being remote makes communication more difficult. It is hard to share my thoughts and communication doesn't go well, then it gets very frustrating. Good thing is I and pairing partner want to get our challenge done. We stay focused extra hour and eventually get our things done. It is very rewarding and gives a good feeling.

After pairing there is feedback time. I learn from pairing session and I get a time to organize what I learned when writing feedback. Some partners are great. Someone is logigal and technically advanced than me. Someone communicates really well and make the session comfortable. Someone who doesn't have this quality they have something I can learn. I recall myself what I did bad at pairing session and how that would affect pairing. This would improve my pairing skill.

The feedback I received was kind and I found few feedback is very helpful. Technical feedback is very clear and specific. It lets me know where I am and provides where I can find document.
Feedback for communication skill is more difficult to give and take. Few good ones totally convinced me and they are specific. I agree how I did and I tried to change next pairing.

I want to write a constructive feedback. But I am afraid I would make my partner feel bad. I ended up writing plain and unhelpful feedback sometimes. My feedback should be specific and honest for partners though.
When I read few constructive feedback I wasn't pleasant. When reading it second time I could take it with less emotion. I guess some would be the same. I will work on writing honest and constructive feedback.
